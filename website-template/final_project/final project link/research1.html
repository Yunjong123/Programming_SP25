Directive Prism: Internal Behavior Shaping Protocol (2016)

Objective

Eclipse Dom's Behavioral Influence Lab initiated Directive Prism to test whether algorithmic sequencing—rather than content—could guide human attention, emotion, and decision-making without direct awareness.

Experiment Structure

  • 1,200 subjects unknowingly enrolled in a "personalized news feed beta"
  • AI tracked user input: scroll speed, hesitation, and dwell time
  • Content order was modified based on emotional reactivity
  • No content was deleted—only the order was manipulated

Findings

  • 84% showed measurable shifts in opinion after 14 days
  • 71% showed increased bias toward emotionally reinforced content
  • Only 4% suspected they were part of an experiment

The success of Directive Prism led to internal discussions regarding long-term integration into major civilian platforms. Leaked documents reference a potential "Phase II" including biometric feedback loops and real-time physiological input.
